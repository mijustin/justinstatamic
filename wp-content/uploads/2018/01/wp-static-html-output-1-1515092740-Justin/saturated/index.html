Is the market for self-published books saturated?

Posted on February 5, 2016 by Justin

Chris Guillebeau just published a blog post about online publishing. It starts out with a confession of sorts:

For the past eight years I’ve made a good living through online publishing. But I don’t think the instructions are as timeless as I thought they were at the time. If, today, you do exactly what I said back then, I’m not entirely sure that success will follow.

One of his key points is that the market has become saturated:

What happens when large numbers of people all pursue the same goal? They all set out to “build a following” and “create platform” for themselves. They all write ebooks. They all create online courses, some more helpful than others.

He continues this line of thinking with a direct warning to new, aspiring self-published authors:

These days, it’s much harder for most people to reap a substantial profit on a new digital product.

Wow.

  2. justinabrahms says:
    February 5, 2016 at 12:02 pm

    The notion that we’ve somehow hit peak book is farcical. Humans like written content. Furthermore, the notion that “If I do what I did 8 years ago, I won’t be as successful” is true for any online industry. Simply put, the web changes quickly and so must your strategies.

    What I think Guillebeau is getting at is that people are copying successful strategies which makes them ineffective. Everyone is trying to “build a list” or “write content upgrades” or “try out some facebook ads”. These aren’t new, but they are saturated. This isn’t unique to marketing. It happens with your favorite little hole-in-the-wall bar, that gets ruined by “bros and hipsters”. Put another way, you’re talking about the gentrification process as applied to online marketing. We’re currently in the “yuppies are ruining the cool art scene” stage.

    Take this as a challenge. What are channels that aren’t saturated? What are people ignoring? Where is your audience? This isn’t different, but you can’t rest on your laurels. Is instagram a channel you’re looking at? Is snapchat? What about peach? Maybe you market by buying ads on someone’s slack bot. Who knows?!

    Online marketing is dead. Long live online marketing.

    There’s a lot of $39/$49 self-published books, targeting the same audience (e.g., freelancers, startups), from people within the same overlapping circles. While each book is different, they do start to overlap each other and there’s only so many books an individual will buy in & around the same subject area. I went through a spate of buying a lot of them but I stopped when I stopped learning.

    I was also very disapppointed when several authors following on from the likes of e.g. Brennan Dunn and Nathan Barry, decided they just needed to write some words and slap a $49 price tag on a book to be successful. Largely the more recent authors haven’t tried as hard to earn their $49 value. $49 is cheap for a great, actionable book which changes your life or business; but $49 is a complete rip-off for some of the poorly written, poorly researched, and derivative books which I’ve seen recently.

    Is the market for self-published books saturated? No, I don’t thing so. But I am pretty disillusioned with it.
